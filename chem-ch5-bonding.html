Chapter 5: Chemical Bonding

📘 SS1 First Term⏱️ Reading: 40 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Explain why atoms form bonds
  • Describe ionic bonding with examples
  • Describe covalent bonding with examples
  • Explain metallic bonding
  • Compare properties of ionic and covalent compounds

5.1 Why Do Atoms Bond?

📌 Octet Rule: Atoms bond to achieve a stable electron configuration of 8 electrons in their outermost shell (like noble gases). They can gain, lose, or share electrons.

Atoms bond to become more stable. The noble gases (Group 18) are already stable with full outer shells, so they rarely form bonds.

5.2 Ionic Bonding

📌 Ionic Bond: The electrostatic force of attraction between oppositely charged ions (cation + anion). Formed when a metal transfers electrons to a non-metal.
📐 Example: NaCl (Sodium Chloride)
Na → Na⁺ + e⁻ (loses 1 electron)
Cl + e⁻ → Cl⁻ (gains 1 electron)
Na⁺ + Cl⁻ → NaCl (ionic bond)
📐 Example: MgO (Magnesium Oxide)
Mg → Mg²⁺ + 2e⁻ (loses 2 electrons)
O + 2e⁻ → O²⁻ (gains 2 electrons)
Mg²⁺ + O²⁻ → MgO

Properties of Ionic Compounds

PropertyExplanation
High melting/boiling pointsStrong electrostatic forces between ions
Soluble in waterWater molecules separate the ions
Conduct electricity when molten/aqueousFree ions can move and carry charge
Crystalline solids at room tempIons arranged in regular lattice
BrittleLayers shift and like charges repel

5.3 Covalent Bonding

📌 Covalent Bond: A bond formed by the sharing of electrons between two non-metal atoms.

Types of Covalent Bonds

TypeElectron Pairs SharedExample
Single bond1 pair (2 electrons)H-H, Cl-Cl, H-Cl
Double bond2 pairs (4 electrons)O=O, CO₂
Triple bond3 pairs (6 electrons)N≡N, C₂H₂
📐 Example: H₂O (Water)
Oxygen shares electrons with 2 hydrogen atoms. O has 2 lone pairs and 2 bonding pairs → bent shape.

Properties of Covalent Compounds

  • Low melting/boiling points (weak intermolecular forces)
  • Do NOT conduct electricity (no free ions or electrons)
  • Often liquids or gases at room temperature
  • Insoluble in water (but soluble in organic solvents)

5.4 Metallic Bonding

📌 Metallic Bond: The electrostatic attraction between positive metal ions and a "sea" of delocalized electrons.

Properties of Metals:

  • ✅ Conduct electricity (delocalized electrons move freely)
  • ✅ Conduct heat
  • ✅ Malleable (can be hammered into sheets)
  • ✅ Ductile (can be drawn into wires)
  • ✅ Shiny (lustrous)
  • ✅ High melting points (strong metallic bonds)

5.5 Comparison: Ionic vs Covalent

PropertyIonicCovalent
Formed betweenMetal + Non-metalNon-metal + Non-metal
ElectronsTransferredShared

📝 Chapter 5 Summary

  • Ionic bond = metal transfers electrons to non-metal (e.g., NaCl)
  • Covalent bond = non-metals share electrons (e.g., H₂O, CO₂)
  • Metallic bond = metal ions + sea of delocalized electrons
  • Ionic: high MP, conduct when molten. Covalent: low MP, don't conduct
