Chapter 18: Environmental Chemistry

📙 SS3 First Term⏱️ Reading: 35 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Identify types of pollution
  • Explain causes of air and water pollution
  • Describe acid rain formation
  • Understand ozone layer depletion
  • Explain the greenhouse effect and climate change

18.1 Types of Pollution

Pollution is the introduction of harmful substances into the environment.

AirVehicles, factories, burningRespiratory diseases, acid rain

18.3 Acid Rain

📌 Acid Rain: Rain with pH < 5.6 caused by SO₂ and NOx dissolving in atmospheric water.

Formation: SO₂ + H₂O → H₂SO₃ (sulfurous acid); 2SO₂ + O₂ + 2H₂O → 2H₂SO₄ (sulfuric acid); 4NO₂ + O₂ + 2H₂O → 4HNO₃ (nitric acid)

Effects: Damages buildings (dissolves limestone), kills aquatic life, leaches soil nutrients, damages forests.

18.4 Ozone Layer Depletion

📌 Ozone (O₃): A layer in the stratosphere that absorbs harmful UV radiation from the Sun.

CFCs (Chlorofluorocarbons) break down ozone: CFCl₃ + UV → CFCl₂• + Cl•; Cl• + O₃ → ClO• + O₂; ClO• + O → Cl• + O₂ (chain reaction — one Cl destroys many O₃ molecules)

Effects: Increased UV → skin cancer, cataracts, reduced crop yields, damage to marine life.

Solutions: Montreal Protocol (1987) — banned CFCs; use alternatives (HCFCs, HFCs).

18.5 Greenhouse Effect & Global Warming

📌 Greenhouse Effect: Trapping of heat by greenhouse gases (CO₂, CH₄, H₂O vapor) in the atmosphere.

Main Greenhouse Gases:

  • CO₂ — burning fossil fuels (largest contributor)
  • CH₄ — agriculture (rice paddies, cattle)
  • N₂O — fertilizers
  • CFCs — refrigerants (also deplete ozone)

Effects: Global temperature rise, melting ice caps, sea level rise, extreme weather events, ecosystem disruption.

18.6 Water Pollution

Sources: Industrial effluents, sewage, agricultural runoff (fertilizers, pesticides), oil spills.

Eutrophication: Excess nutrients (N, P) from fertilizers → algal bloom → oxygen depletion → death of aquatic life.

18.7 Green Chemistry

Principles: Prevent waste, use renewable feedstocks, design safer chemicals, use catalysts, reduce energy consumption, minimize pollution at the source.

📝 Chapter 18 Summary

  • Air pollutants: CO₂, CO, SO₂, NOx, particulates, CFCs
  • Acid rain: SO₂+NOx → H₂SO₄+HNO₃ (pH<5.6)
  • Ozone depletion: CFCs destroy O₃ → increased UV
  • Greenhouse effect: CO₂, CH₄ trap heat → global warming
  • Green chemistry: Sustainable, less waste, safer chemicals
