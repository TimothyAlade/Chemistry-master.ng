Chapter 4: The Periodic Table

📘 SS1 First Term⏱️ Reading: 35 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Understand the history of the periodic table
  • Identify groups and periods
  • Explain periodic trends
  • Describe properties of key groups

4.1 History of the Periodic Table

📌 Dmitri Mendeleev (1869): Arranged elements by increasing atomic mass, leaving gaps for undiscovered elements. The modern periodic table arranges elements by increasing atomic number.

Mendeleev's Predictions: He predicted the existence and properties of gallium (eka-aluminum), scandium (eka-boron), and germanium (eka-silicon) before they were discovered!

4.2 Structure of the Periodic Table

FeatureDescription
Groups (Columns)Vertical columns (1-18). Elements in same group have similar chemical properties because they have the same number of valence electrons.
Periods (Rows)Horizontal rows (1-7). Properties change gradually across a period.
💡 Memory Tip: Groups go down ↓ (same properties). Periods go across → (properties change).

4.3 Key Groups in the Periodic Table

GroupNamePropertiesExamples
1Alkali MetalsSoft, reactive, 1 valence electronLi, Na, K
2Alkaline Earth MetalsLess reactive than Group 1, 2 valence electronsMg, Ca, Ba
7 (17)HalogensVery reactive non-metals, 7 valence electronsF, Cl, Br, I
8 (18)Noble GasesUnreactive (inert), full outer shellHe, Ne, Ar, Kr
TransitionTransition MetalsForm colored compounds, multiple oxidation statesFe, Cu, Zn, Ag, Au

4.4 Periodic Trends

Across a Period (Left → Right)

  • Atomic radius: DECREASES (more protons pull electrons closer)
  • Ionization energy: INCREASES (harder to remove electrons)
  • Electronegativity: INCREASES
  • Metallic character: DECREASES

Down a Group (Top → Bottom)

  • Atomic radius: INCREASES (more electron shells)
  • Ionization energy: DECREASES (easier to remove electrons)
  • Reactivity (metals): INCREASES
  • Reactivity (non-metals): DECREASES

📝 Chapter 4 Summary

  • Modern periodic table arranged by atomic number
  • Groups = vertical (similar properties); Periods = horizontal
  • Key groups: Alkali Metals (1), Halogens (17), Noble Gases (18)
  • Across period: radius decreases, ionization energy increases
  • Down group: radius increases, ionization energy decreases
