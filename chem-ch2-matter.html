Chapter 2: Matter and Separation Techniques

📘 SS1 First Term⏱️ Reading: 35 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Define matter and its states
  • Distinguish between physical and chemical changes
  • Differentiate elements, compounds, and mixtures
  • Describe separation techniques

2.1 States of Matter

📌 Matter: Anything that has mass and occupies space. Exists in three states: Solid, Liquid, and Gas.
PropertySolidLiquidGas
ShapeFixedTakes container's shapeFills container
VolumeFixedFixedVariable
CompressibilityVery lowLowHigh
Particle arrangementTightly packedClose, mobileFar apart
🔄 Changes of State: Melting (S→L), Freezing (L→S), Boiling (L→G), Condensation (G→L), Sublimation (S→G)

2.2 Physical and Chemical Changes

Physical ChangeChemical Change
No new substance formedNew substance formed
Easily reversibleDifficult to reverse
Examples: melting ice, boiling waterBurning wood, rusting iron, cooking
No energy change usuallyEnergy released or absorbed

2.3 Elements, Compounds, and Mixtures

ElementCompoundMixture
One type of atomTwo+ elements chemically combinedTwo+ substances physically mixed
Cannot be broken downCan be broken chemicallyCan be separated physically
Examples: Fe, O₂, NaH₂O, NaCl, CO₂Air, salt water, soil

2.4 Separation Techniques

1. Filtration

Separates insoluble solids from liquids. Example: Sand from water, tea leaves from tea.

2. Distillation

Separates liquids with different boiling points. Example: Pure water from salt water, crude oil refining.

📐 Simple Distillation: Heat the mixture → liquid with lower BP boils first → vapor condenses → collected as distillate.

3. Chromatography

Separates dissolved substances based on their movement through a medium. Example: Separating ink pigments, drug testing.

4. Crystallization

Obtaining pure solid crystals from a solution by evaporating the solvent. Example: Salt from seawater, sugar crystals.

5. Magnetic Separation

Uses magnets to separate magnetic from non-magnetic materials. Example: Iron filings from sand.

6. Decantation

Carefully pouring off liquid, leaving solid behind. Example: Water from settled sand.

📝 Chapter 2 Summary

  • 3 states of matter: Solid, Liquid, Gas
  • Physical change = no new substance; Chemical change = new substance
  • Elements = one atom type; Compounds = chemically combined; Mixtures = physically mixed
  • 6 separation techniques: Filtration, Distillation, Chromatography, Crystallization, Magnetic, Decantation
