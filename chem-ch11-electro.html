Chapter 11: Electrochemistry

📗 SS2 Second Term⏱️ Reading: 40 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Define electrolysis and electrolytes
  • Explain Faraday's laws of electrolysis
  • Describe electrochemical cells
  • Distinguish electrolytic vs galvanic cells

11.1 Electrolysis

📌 Electrolysis: The decomposition of a compound using direct electric current. It occurs in an electrolytic cell with electrodes (anode +, cathode −) dipped in an electrolyte.

11.2 Key Terms

TermDefinition
ElectrolyteSubstance that conducts electricity when molten/aqueous
AnodePositive electrode — attracts anions (oxidation occurs)
CathodeNegative electrode — attracts cations (reduction occurs)
AnionNegative ion — moves to anode
CationPositive ion — moves to cathode

11.3 Faraday's Laws

📌 First Law: Mass deposited ∝ quantity of electricity passed. m = ZIt
📌 Second Law: When same quantity of electricity passes through different electrolytes, masses deposited are proportional to their chemical equivalents.

11.4 Applications of Electrolysis

  • Electroplating: Coating objects with metal (e.g., silver plating)
  • Extraction of metals: Aluminum from bauxite, sodium from NaCl
  • Purification of metals: Copper refining
  • Manufacture of chemicals: NaOH, Cl₂, H₂ from brine

11.5 Electrochemical Cells

Electrolytic CellGalvanic (Voltaic) Cell
Electrical → Chemical energyChemical → Electrical energy
Non-spontaneous reactionSpontaneous reaction
Requires external powerProduces electricity
Example: Electrolysis of waterExample: Daniel cell, battery

📝 Chapter 11 Summary

  • Electrolysis: decomposition by electricity
  • Anode (+): oxidation; Cathode (−): reduction
  • Faraday's 1st Law: m = ZIt
  • Applications: electroplating, metal extraction
