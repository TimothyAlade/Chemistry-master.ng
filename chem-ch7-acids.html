Chapter 7: Acids, Bases, and Salts

📘 SS1 First Term⏱️ Reading: 35 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Define acids and bases
  • Understand the pH scale
  • Describe neutralization reactions
  • Explain methods of salt preparation
  • Identify common acids, bases, and salts

7.1 Acids

📌 Acid: A substance that produces hydrogen ions (H⁺) in aqueous solution. Has pH < 7.
Strong AcidsWeak Acids
HCl (Hydrochloric), H₂SO₄ (Sulfuric), HNO₃ (Nitric)CH₃COOH (Ethanoic/Acetic), H₂CO₃ (Carbonic), Citric acid

Properties of Acids:

  • Sour taste (e.g., lemon, vinegar)
  • Turn blue litmus red
  • React with metals to produce H₂ gas
  • React with bases to form salt + water

7.2 Bases and Alkalis

📌 Base: A substance that neutralizes acids. Alkali: A soluble base that produces OH⁻ ions in water. pH > 7.
Strong AlkalisWeak Alkalis
NaOH (Sodium hydroxide), KOH (Potassium hydroxide)NH₄OH (Ammonium hydroxide), Ca(OH)₂ (Lime water)

Properties:

  • Bitter taste, soapy feel
  • Turn red litmus blue
  • React with acids → salt + water

7.3 The pH Scale

pH RangeTypeExamples
0-2Strong acidBattery acid, stomach acid
3-6Weak acidVinegar, lemon juice
7NeutralPure water, salt solution
8-11Weak alkaliSoap, baking soda
12-14Strong alkaliDrain cleaner, NaOH

7.4 Neutralization

📌 Neutralization: Acid + Base → Salt + Water. This is an exothermic reaction.
📐 Examples:
HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O
H₂SO₄ + 2KOH → K₂SO₄ + 2H₂O

7.5 Preparation of Salts

MethodUsed ForExample
Acid + MetalSoluble saltsZn + H₂SO₄ → ZnSO₄ + H₂
Acid + BaseSoluble saltsHCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O
Acid + CarbonateSoluble salts2HCl + CaCO₃ → CaCl₂ + H₂O + CO₂
PrecipitationInsoluble saltsAgNO₃ + NaCl → AgCl↓ + NaNO₃

7.6 Common Salts and Their Uses

SaltFormulaUse
Sodium chlorideNaClTable salt, food preservation
Calcium carbonateCaCO₃Chalk, limestone, antacid
Sodium bicarbonateNaHCO₃Baking soda, antacid
Copper sulfateCuSO₄Fungicide, electroplating
Ammonium nitrateNH₄NO₃Fertilizer

📝 Chapter 7 Summary

  • Acids: pH < 7, produce H⁺, turn litmus red
  • Bases/Alkalis: pH > 7, produce OH⁻, turn litmus blue
  • Neutralization: Acid + Base → Salt + Water
  • Salts prepared by: acid+metal, acid+base, precipitation
