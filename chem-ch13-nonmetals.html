Chapter 13: Non-Metals

📗 SS2 Second Term⏱️ Reading: 40 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Describe properties of key non-metals
  • Explain the nitrogen cycle
  • Understand the halogens and their reactions
  • Describe carbon and its compounds

13.1 Hydrogen (H₂)

Properties: Lightest element, colorless, odorless, highly flammable. Preparation: Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂

Uses: Ammonia production (Haber process), rocket fuel, hydrogenation of oils, fuel cells.

13.2 Oxygen (O₂)

Preparation: 2H₂O₂ →(MnO₂ catalyst)→ 2H₂O + O₂; 2KClO₃ →(heat)→ 2KCl + 3O₂

Uses: Respiration, combustion, steel making, medical oxygen, welding.

13.3 Nitrogen and the Nitrogen Cycle

📌 Nitrogen Cycle: The circulation of nitrogen between the atmosphere, soil, plants, and animals.

Steps: Nitrogen fixation (N₂ → NH₃/NH₄⁺) → Nitrification (NH₄⁺ → NO₂⁻ → NO₃⁻) → Assimilation (plants absorb NO₃⁻) → Ammonification (decay) → Denitrification (NO₃⁻ → N₂)

13.4 Halogens (Group 17)

ElementStateColorReactivity
Fluorine (F₂)GasPale yellowMost reactive
Chlorine (Cl₂)GasGreenish-yellowVery reactive
Bromine (Br₂)LiquidRed-brownModerately reactive
Iodine (I₂)SolidPurple-blackLeast reactive

Reactivity decreases DOWN the group. A more reactive halogen displaces a less reactive one: Cl₂ + 2KBr → 2KCl + Br₂

13.5 Carbon and Its Oxides

Carbon dioxide (CO₂): Turns limewater milky. Ca(OH)₂ + CO₂ → CaCO₃ + H₂O

Carbon monoxide (CO): Toxic, colorless, odorless. Binds to hemoglobin, preventing oxygen transport.

13.6 Sulfur

Uses: Manufacture of H₂SO₄ (Contact process), vulcanization of rubber, fungicides, gunpowder.

📝 Chapter 13 Summary

  • Hydrogen: lightest, flammable, used in Haber process
  • Oxygen: supports combustion, prepared from H₂O₂
  • Nitrogen cycle: fixation → nitrification → assimilation → denitrification
  • Halogens: F>Cl>Br>I in reactivity
