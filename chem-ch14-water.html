Chapter 14: Water & Solutions

📗 SS2 Second Term⏱️ Reading: 35 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Distinguish hard and soft water
  • Explain water purification methods
  • Define solubility and factors affecting it
  • Interpret solubility curves
  • Understand water treatment

14.1 Hard vs Soft Water

📌 Hard Water: Water that does NOT lather easily with soap due to dissolved Ca²⁺ and Mg²⁺ ions.
📌 Soft Water: Lathers easily with soap.
Temporary HardnessPermanent Hardness
Caused by Ca(HCO₃)₂, Mg(HCO₃)₂Caused by CaSO₄, MgSO₄, CaCl₂
Removed by boilingNOT removed by boiling
Ca(HCO₃)₂ → CaCO₃↓ + H₂O + CO₂Requires chemical treatment

14.2 Removing Hardness

  • Boiling: Only for temporary hardness
  • Adding washing soda (Na₂CO₃): Precipitates Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺ as carbonates
  • Ion exchange (Permutit): Replaces Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺ with Na⁺ using zeolite
  • Distillation: Produces pure water (expensive)
📐 Washing Soda Reaction:
CaSO₄ + Na₂CO₃ → CaCO₃↓ + Na₂SO₄

14.3 Water Purification (Treatment)

  1. Filtration: Removes large particles (leaves, debris)
  2. Sedimentation: Particles settle to bottom
  3. Coagulation: Alum (Al₂(SO₄)₃) added to clump fine particles
  4. Filtration through sand/gravel: Removes remaining particles
  5. Chlorination: Cl₂ added to kill bacteria and pathogens
  6. Fluoridation: NaF added to prevent tooth decay (optional)

14.4 Solubility

📌 Solubility: The maximum mass of solute that dissolves in 100g of solvent at a given temperature.

Factors Affecting Solubility

FactorEffect on SolidsEffect on Gases
Temperature ↑Usually increasesDecreases
Pressure ↑No effectIncreases (Henry's Law)
Nature of solute/solvent"Like dissolves like"—
Surface area ↑Faster dissolving (not more)—

14.5 Solubility Curves

A solubility curve is a graph showing how solubility changes with temperature.

  • Above the curve: Supersaturated solution
  • On the curve: Saturated solution
  • Below the curve: Unsaturated solution
💡 Note: Most solids show increasing solubility with temperature, but gases show decreasing solubility.

14.6 Types of Solutions

TypeDefinition
UnsaturatedMore solute can still dissolve
SaturatedNo more solute can dissolve at that temperature
SupersaturatedContains more solute than normally possible (unstable)
DiluteSmall amount of solute relative to solvent
ConcentratedLarge amount of solute relative to solvent

14.7 Advantages and Disadvantages of Hard Water

Advantages ✅Disadvantages ❌
Provides calcium for bones/teethWastes soap (forms scum)
Tastes better than soft waterScale in kettles and pipes
Used in brewing beerDamages industrial boilers

📝 Chapter 14 Summary

  • Hard water: Ca²⁺/Mg²⁺ ions, doesn't lather with soap
  • Temporary hardness: removed by boiling (Ca(HCO₃)₂ → CaCO₃↓)
  • Permanent hardness: needs Na₂CO₃ or ion exchange
  • Solubility = max solute per 100g solvent at given temperature
  • Purification: Filtration → Sedimentation → Coagulation → Chlorination
