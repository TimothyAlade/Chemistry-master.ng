15.1 Alcohols (R-OH)

📌 Alcohols: Organic compounds containing the hydroxyl (-OH) functional group attached to a saturated carbon atom. General formula: CₙH₂ₙ₊₁OH.

Common Alcohols

NameMolecular FormulaStructural FormulaUses
MethanolCH₃OHCH₃-OHSolvent, fuel, antifreeze
EthanolC₂H₅OHCH₃CH₂-OHAlcoholic drinks, fuel, solvent, disinfectant
PropanolC₃H₇OHCH₃CH₂CH₂-OHDisinfectant, solvent
ButanolC₄H₉OHCH₃CH₂CH₂CH₂-OHPaint remover, solvent

Preparation of Ethanol

Method 1: Fermentation

C₆H₁₂O₆ →(yeast, 37°C)→ 2C₂H₅OH + 2CO₂

Glucose is fermented by yeast enzymes under anaerobic conditions at about 37°C. The ethanol produced is about 12-15% concentration, which can be increased by fractional distillation.

Method 2: Hydration of Ethene

C₂H₄ + H₂O →(H₃PO₄, 300°C, 60atm)→ C₂H₅OH

Reactions of Ethanol

  • Combustion: C₂H₅OH + 3O₂ → 2CO₂ + 3H₂O (clean flame)
  • Oxidation: C₂H₅OH + [O] → CH₃CHO (ethanal) → CH₃COOH (ethanoic acid)
  • Dehydration: C₂H₅OH →(H₂SO₄, 170°C)→ C₂H₄ + H₂O
  • Esterification: Reacts with carboxylic acids to form esters

15.2 Carboxylic Acids (R-COOH)

📌 Carboxylic Acids: Organic compounds containing the carboxyl (-COOH) functional group. They are weak acids with the general formula CₙH₂ₙ₊₁COOH.
NameFormulaFound InUses
Methanoic acidHCOOHAnt stings, nettlesPreservative, textile processing
Ethanoic acidCH₃COOHVinegar (5-8%)Food preservative, solvent, vinegar
Propanoic acidC₂H₅COOHDairy productsFood preservative, herbicide
Citric acidC₆H₈O₇Citrus fruitsFood flavoring, cleaning agent
Lactic acidC₃H₆O₃Sour milk, musclesFood preservative, cosmetics

Properties of Carboxylic Acids

  • Weak acids (partially dissociate in water)
  • React with metals to produce H₂ gas
  • React with bases to form salts
  • React with alcohols to form esters (esterification)
  • Have higher boiling points than corresponding alcohols (due to hydrogen bonding)
📐 Reaction with Sodium:
2CH₃COOH + 2Na → 2CH₃COONa + H₂
(Ethanoic acid + Sodium → Sodium ethanoate + Hydrogen)

15.3 Esters (R-COO-R')

📌 Esters: Organic compounds formed by the reaction of a carboxylic acid with an alcohol. They have the general formula R-COO-R' and are characterized by their pleasant, fruity smells.

Esterification

Carboxylic Acid + Alcohol ⇌ Ester + Water

The reaction is reversible and requires an acid catalyst (usually concentrated H₂SO₄).

📐 Example 1:
CH₃COOH + C₂H₅OH ⇌ CH₃COOC₂H₅ + H₂O
Ethanoic acid + Ethanol ⇌ Ethyl ethanoate + Water
(Smells like pear drops/nail polish remover)
📐 Example 2:
HCOOH + CH₃OH ⇌ HCOOCH₃ + H₂O
Methanoic acid + Methanol ⇌ Methyl methanoate + Water

Uses of Esters

  • Perfumes and fragrances: Pleasant fruity smells
  • Food flavorings: Artificial fruit flavors (banana, apple, pineapple)
  • Solvents: Nail polish remover (ethyl ethanoate), paint thinners
  • Biodiesel: Methyl esters of fatty acids
  • Plasticizers: Making plastics flexible

15.4 Saponification (Soap Making)

📌 Saponification: The alkaline hydrolysis of fats and oils (triglycerides) to produce soap (sodium/potassium salts of fatty acids) and glycerol.
Fat/Oil + NaOH → Soap + Glycerol
📐 Example:
Glyceryl tristearate (fat) + 3NaOH → 3C₁₇H₃₅COONa (sodium stearate - soap) + C₃H₅(OH)₃ (glycerol)

How Soap Works

Soap molecules have a hydrophilic (water-loving) head and a hydrophobic (water-hating) tail. The hydrophobic tail dissolves in grease/oil, while the hydrophilic head dissolves in water, forming micelles that lift dirt away.

Soap vs Detergent

SoapDetergent
Made from natural fats/oilsMade from petroleum products
Forms scum in hard waterWorks well in hard water
BiodegradableSome are non-biodegradable
Example: Sodium stearateExample: Sodium lauryl sulfate

15.5 Polymers and Polymerization

📌 Polymer: A large molecule made by joining many small repeating units (monomers) together.
📌 Polymerization: The process of joining monomers to form a polymer.

Types of Polymerization

Addition PolymerizationCondensation Polymerization
Monomers join directly (C=C double bond opens)Monomers join with loss of small molecule (usually H₂O)
No by-product formedSmall molecule eliminated (H₂O, HCl, NH₃)
Example: Polyethene from etheneExample: Nylon, polyester, proteins
n(CH₂=CH₂) → [-CH₂-CH₂-]ₙDiamine + Dicarboxylic acid → Nylon + H₂O

🧪 Thermosoftening vs Thermosetting:
• Thermosoftening (Thermoplastic): Softens when heated, can be remolded (e.g., polyethene, PVC)
• Thermosetting: Does NOT soften when heated, cannot be remolded (e.g., bakelite, melamine)

15.6 Proteins and Amino Acids

📌 Amino Acids: Building blocks of proteins. Contain both -NH₂ (amine) and -COOH (carboxyl) groups. There are 20 naturally occurring amino acids.
H₂N-CHR-COOH

Amino acids link through peptide bonds (-CONH-) to form proteins (polypeptides).

📝 Chapter 15 Summary

  • Alcohols contain -OH group; ethanol (C₂H₅OH) made by fermentation
  • Carboxylic acids contain -COOH; ethanoic acid (CH₃COOH) is vinegar
  • Esters (R-COO-R') formed by acid + alcohol; have fruity smells; used in perfumes
  • Saponification: Fat + NaOH → Soap + Glycerol
  • Addition polymerization: Monomers join directly (polyethene from ethene)
  • Condensation polymerization: Monomers join with loss of H₂O (nylon, polyester)
  • Natural polymers: Starch, protein, DNA
