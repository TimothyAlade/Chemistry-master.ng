Chapter 16: Nuclear Chemistry

📙 SS3 First Term⏱️ Reading: 40 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Define radioactivity and types of radiation
  • Distinguish alpha, beta, and gamma radiation
  • Calculate half-life problems
  • Explain nuclear fission and fusion
  • Describe uses and dangers of radioactivity

16.1 What is Radioactivity?

📌 Radioactivity: The spontaneous emission of radiation from unstable atomic nuclei. Discovered by Henri Becquerel (1896) and studied by Marie and Pierre Curie.

16.3 Nuclear Equations

📐 Alpha Decay: ²³⁸₉₂U → ²³⁴₉₀Th + ⁴₂He (α)
📐 Beta Decay: ¹⁴₆C → ¹⁴₇N + ⁰₋₁e (β)
📐 Gamma Emission: ⁶⁰₂₇Co* → ⁶⁰₂₇Co + γ

16.4 Half-Life (T½)

📌 Half-Life: The time taken for half the radioactive nuclei in a sample to decay.
T½ = ln(2)/λ = 0.693/λ
N = N₀ × (½)^(t/T½)
📐 Example: A sample has half-life of 5 years. After 20 years, what fraction remains?
Number of half-lives = 20/5 = 4
Fraction remaining = (½)⁴ = 1/16

16.5 Nuclear Fission

📌 Nuclear Fission: Splitting of a heavy nucleus (e.g., U-235) into two lighter nuclei, releasing enormous energy and neutrons. Used in nuclear power plants and atomic bombs.
📐 Example: ²³⁵₉₂U + ¹₀n → ¹⁴¹₅₆Ba + ⁹²₃₆Kr + 3¹₀n + Energy

16.6 Nuclear Fusion

📌 Nuclear Fusion: Combining of two light nuclei (e.g., hydrogen isotopes) to form a heavier nucleus, releasing even more energy than fission. Powers the Sun and stars.
📐 Example: ²₁H + ³₁H → ⁴₂He + ¹₀n + Energy

16.7 Uses of Radioactivity

  • Medicine: Radiotherapy (Co-60), PET scans, sterilization
  • Archaeology: Carbon-14 dating (determining age of fossils)
  • Industry: Thickness gauges, smoke detectors (Am-241), leak detection
  • Energy: Nuclear power plants
  • Agriculture: Food preservation (irradiation), pest control

16.8 Dangers and Safety

  • Radiation sickness: Nausea, vomiting, hair loss
  • Cancer: DNA damage from ionizing radiation
  • Genetic mutations: Can affect future generations
  • Safety: Shielding (lead), limit exposure time, maintain distance, use remote handling

📝 Chapter 16 Summary

  • α (He nucleus, +2, low penetration), β (electron, −1, medium), γ (EM, 0, high)
  • Half-life: T½ = 0.693/λ; N = N₀(½)^(t/T½)
  • Fission: Splitting heavy nuclei (U-235); Fusion: Combining light nuclei (H→He)
  • Uses: Medicine (radiotherapy), archaeology (C-14 dating), energy
