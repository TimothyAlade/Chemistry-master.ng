Chapter 12: Metals & Metallurgy

📗 SS2 Second Term⏱️ Reading: 40 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Understand the reactivity series of metals
  • Describe extraction of iron, aluminum, and copper
  • Explain alloy formation
  • Understand corrosion and its prevention

12.1 Reactivity Series

📌 Reactivity Series: K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Sn > Pb > (H) > Cu > Hg > Ag > Au > Pt
💡 Memory Trick: "Kings Never Carry Makeup After Zebra In The Lead House, Come Here Silver And Gold Please"

12.2 Extraction of Iron (Blast Furnace)

Raw Materials: Iron ore (Fe₂O₃), Coke (C), Limestone (CaCO₃)

Reactions:

  • C + O₂ → CO₂ (coke burns)
  • CO₂ + C → 2CO (reducing agent)
  • Fe₂O₃ + 3CO → 2Fe + 3CO₂ (iron reduced)
  • CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂ (limestone decomposes)
  • CaO + SiO₂ → CaSiO₃ (slag formation)

12.3 Extraction of Aluminum (Hall-Héroult Process)

Aluminum extracted by electrolysis of molten alumina (Al₂O₃) dissolved in cryolite (Na₃AlF₆).

12.4 Alloys

AlloyCompositionUse
SteelFe + CConstruction, tools
BrassCu + ZnMusical instruments
BronzeCu + SnStatues, coins
DuraluminAl + Cu + MgAircraft bodies
SolderPb + SnJoining metals

12.5 Corrosion and Prevention

📌 Rusting: Fe + O₂ + H₂O → Fe₂O₃·xH₂O (hydrated iron(III) oxide)

Prevention Methods:

  • Painting/oiling
  • Galvanizing (coating with zinc)
  • Sacrificial protection
  • Alloying (stainless steel)
  • Electroplating

📝 Chapter 12 Summary

  • Reactivity: K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Cu > Au
  • Iron: Blast furnace (coke, limestone, iron ore)
  • Aluminum: Electrolysis of Al₂O₃ in cryolite
  • Rusting needs O₂ + H₂O; prevented by painting, galvanizing
