17.1 Haber Process (Ammonia Production)

📌 Haber Process: Industrial production of ammonia (NH₃) from nitrogen and hydrogen.
N₂(g) + 3H₂(g) ⇌ 2NH₃(g)   ΔH = −92 kJ/mol
ConditionValueReason
Temperature450°CCompromise: fast rate + reasonable yield
Pressure200 atmFavors products (4→2 moles of gas)
CatalystIron (Fe)Speeds up reaction without affecting equilibrium

Uses of Ammonia: Fertilizers (80% of production), nitric acid, explosives, cleaning products, refrigeration.

17.2 Contact Process (Sulfuric Acid)

📌 Contact Process: Industrial production of sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄) — the most produced chemical worldwide.

Step 1: S + O₂ → SO₂ (burning sulfur in air)

Step 2: 2SO₂ + O₂ ⇌ 2SO₃ (V₂O₅ catalyst, 450°C, 1-2 atm)

Step 3: SO₃ + H₂SO₄ → H₂S₂O₇ (oleum — avoids acid mist)

Step 4: H₂S₂O₇ + H₂O → 2H₂SO₄

Uses: Fertilizers, car batteries, detergents, metal processing, petroleum refining.

17.3 Cement Manufacturing

Raw Materials: Limestone (CaCO₃) + Clay (SiO₂, Al₂O₃)

  1. Crushed and mixed with water → slurry
  2. Heated in rotary kiln at 1400-1500°C → clinker
  3. Clinker ground with gypsum (CaSO₄·2H₂O) → cement

Setting of Cement: Cement + Water → Hydrated calcium silicates (hardens over time)

17.4 Glass Manufacturing

Raw Materials: Sand (SiO₂), Sodium carbonate (Na₂CO₃), Calcium carbonate (CaCO₃)

Process: Mixture heated to 1500°C → molten glass → cooled and shaped

TypeCompositionUse
Soda-lime glassNa₂O·CaO·6SiO₂Windows, bottles
Borosilicate glassSiO₂ + B₂O₃Lab glassware (Pyrex)
Lead crystalSiO₂ + PbODecorative items

17.5 Fertilizers

NPK Fertilizers contain Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), and Potassium (K).

FertilizerFormulaElement Supplied
Ammonium nitrateNH₄NO₃N (33%)
UreaCO(NH₂)₂N (46%)
SuperphosphateCa(H₂PO₄)₂P
Potassium chlorideKClK

17.6 Soap and Detergent Manufacturing

Soap: Fat/Oil + NaOH (boiling) → Soap + Glycerol (saponification)

Detergent: Petroleum fractions + H₂SO₄ → sulfonic acids → neutralized with NaOH

📝 Chapter 17 Summary

  • Haber: N₂+3H₂⇌2NH₃ (450°C, 200atm, Fe catalyst)
  • Contact: SO₂→SO₃→H₂S₂O₇→H₂SO₄ (V₂O₅ catalyst)
  • Cement: Limestone+Clay (1400°C)→Clinker+Gypsum
  • Glass: Sand+Na₂CO₃+CaCO₃ (1500°C)
