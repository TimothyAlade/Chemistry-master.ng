Chapter 1: Introduction to Chemistry

📘 SS1 First Term ⏱️ Reading: 25 mins 📖 Sections: 5

🎯 Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you should be able to:

  • Define chemistry and explain its scope
  • Identify and describe the main branches of chemistry
  • Explain the importance of chemistry in everyday life
  • Describe the scientific method
  • Understand laboratory safety rules

1.1 What is Chemistry?

📌 Definition: Chemistry is the branch of science that studies the composition, structure, properties, and changes of matter. It deals with how substances interact, combine, and change.

The word "Chemistry" comes from the ancient practice of alchemy. Chemistry is often called the "central science" because it connects physics with biology, medicine, and engineering.

Chemistry answers questions like:

  • What is water made of?
  • Why does iron rust?
  • How do batteries produce electricity?
  • What makes food cook faster with salt?
  • How are medicines made?

1.2 Branches of Chemistry

Chemistry is broadly divided into several branches:

BranchWhat It StudiesExamples
Organic ChemistryCarbon-containing compoundsPlastics, petrol, drugs, proteins
Inorganic ChemistryNon-carbon compoundsMetals, salts, minerals, acids
Physical ChemistryPhysics of chemical systemsThermodynamics, kinetics, electrochemistry
Analytical ChemistryAnalysis and measurementWater quality testing, drug testing
BiochemistryChemical processes in living thingsDNA, enzymes, hormones, metabolism
Industrial ChemistryLarge-scale chemical productionFertilizers, cement, paints
💡 Remember: Organic = Carbon compounds | Inorganic = Everything else

1.3 Importance of Chemistry in Everyday Life

🏠 At Home

  • Cooking: Chemical reactions occur when food is heated
  • Cleaning: Soaps and detergents work through chemical processes
  • Food preservation: Salt, sugar, and refrigeration prevent spoilage

🏥 Medicine

  • Drugs: Paracetamol, antibiotics, antimalarials
  • Vaccines: Chemical formulations that prevent diseases
  • Diagnosis: Blood tests, urine analysis

🌾 Agriculture

  • Fertilizers: NPK (Nitrogen, Phosphorus, Potassium)
  • Pesticides: Protect crops from insects and diseases
  • Herbicides: Control unwanted plants

🏭 Industry

  • Plastics: Polyethylene, PVC, nylon
  • Textiles: Dyes, synthetic fibers
  • Construction: Cement, paints, glass
  • Energy: Petroleum refining, batteries
💡 Key Point: Chemistry is everywhere! From the food you eat to the phone in your hand, chemistry makes modern life possible.

1.4 The Scientific Method

Chemistry, like all sciences, follows a systematic approach called the scientific method:

StepDescriptionExample
1. ObservationNotice a phenomenonIron nails left outside rust
2. QuestionAsk why/howWhy does iron rust?
3. HypothesisPropose an explanationRusting requires air and water
4. ExperimentTest the hypothesisPlace nails in different conditions
5. AnalysisExamine resultsNails rust only with both air and water
6. ConclusionAccept or reject hypothesisRusting needs O₂ and H₂O

1.5 Laboratory Safety Rules

When working in a chemistry laboratory, always follow these safety rules:

  • 👁️ Wear safety goggles to protect your eyes
  • 🧥 Wear a lab coat to protect your clothes and skin
  • 🚫 Never eat or drink in the laboratory
  • 👃 Never smell chemicals directly — waft the fumes toward you
  • ⚠️ Read labels carefully before using any chemical
  • 🧤 Wear gloves when handling corrosive substances
  • 🧯 Know where the fire extinguisher is
  • 🧹 Clean up spills immediately
⚠️ Common Lab Hazard Symbols:
☠️ Toxic/Poisonous | 🔥 Flammable | 💥 Explosive | ⚡ Corrosive | ☢️ Radioactive | ❗ Irritant

📝 Chapter 1 Summary

  • Chemistry is the study of matter, its properties, and changes
  • 6 main branches: Organic, Inorganic, Physical, Analytical, Biochemistry, Industrial
  • Chemistry is essential in medicine, agriculture, industry, and homes
  • The scientific method provides a systematic approach to discovery
  • Lab safety is crucial — always follow safety rules
