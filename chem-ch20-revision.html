📝 20 WAEC/NECO Practice Questions

Q1: Define an element, compound, and mixture with examples.
Answer: Element = pure substance of one atom type (Fe, O₂). Compound = 2+ elements chemically combined (H₂O, NaCl). Mixture = physically mixed substances (air, salt water).
Q2: Calculate the number of moles in 80g of NaOH (Na=23, O=16, H=1).
Answer: NaOH = 23+16+1 = 40 g/mol. n = 80/40 = 2 moles.
Q3: Balance: Fe + HCl → FeCl₂ + H₂
Answer: Fe + 2HCl → FeCl₂ + H₂
Q4: State the Haber process conditions.
Answer: N₂+3H₂⇌2NH₃. 450°C, 200 atm, iron catalyst.
Q5: What is the pH of 0.01M HCl?
Answer: HCl→H⁺+Cl⁻, [H⁺]=0.01M. pH = -log(0.01) = 2.
Q6: Name the first 10 elements of the periodic table.
Answer: H, He, Li, Be, B, C, N, O, F, Ne.
Q7: Distinguish ionic and covalent bonds.
Answer: Ionic = electron transfer (metal+non-metal, e.g., NaCl). Covalent = electron sharing (non-metals, e.g., H₂O).
Q8: Write the formula of aluminum oxide.
Answer: Al³⁺ + O²⁻ → cross multiply → Al₂O₃.
Q9: What is the test for carbon dioxide?
Answer: Bubble through limewater → turns milky. Ca(OH)₂+CO₂→CaCO₃↓+H₂O.
Q10: Explain esterification with an example.
Answer: Acid+Alcohol⇌Ester+Water. CH₃COOH+C₂H₅OH⇌CH₃COOC₂H₅+H₂O.
Q11: What is the difference between temporary and permanent hard water?
Answer: Temporary = Ca(HCO₃)₂, removed by boiling. Permanent = CaSO₄/MgSO₄, not removed by boiling.
Q12: List the three types of radiation.
Answer: Alpha (⁴₂He, +2, paper), Beta (⁰₋₁e, -1, Al), Gamma (EM, 0, lead).
Q13: What is a catalyst? Give an example.
Answer: Substance that speeds reaction without being consumed. Example: MnO₂ in H₂O₂ decomposition.
Q14: State Le Chatelier's Principle.
Answer: System at equilibrium shifts to oppose any change in conditions.
Q15: Calculate the relative molecular mass of H₂SO₄ (H=1, S=32, O=16).
Answer: (2×1)+32+(4×16) = 2+32+64 = 98.
Q16: What is the difference between fission and fusion?
Answer: Fission = splitting heavy nucleus (U-235). Fusion = combining light nuclei (H→He).
Q17: Name three alloys and their compositions.
Answer: Steel (Fe+C), Brass (Cu+Zn), Bronze (Cu+Sn).
Q18: Write the equation for fermentation of glucose.
Answer: C₆H₁₂O₆→(yeast)→2C₂H₅OH+2CO₂.
Q19: How is rusting prevented?
Answer: Painting, oiling, galvanizing (zinc), sacrificial protection, alloying (stainless steel).
Q20: Calculate the empirical formula of a compound with 40% C, 6.7% H, 53.3% O.
Answer: C:40/12=3.33, H:6.7/1=6.7, O:53.3/16=3.33. Ratio=1:2:1 → CH₂O.

⚠️ Common Exam Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Forgetting to balance equations before calculations
  • ❌ Confusing atomic number (Z) with mass number (A)
  • ❌ Using °C instead of Kelvin in gas law calculations (K = °C + 273)
  • ❌ Forgetting units (mol, g, dm³, etc.)
  • ❌ Not converting cm³ to dm³ (÷1000)
  • ❌ Confusing oxidation (loss of electrons) with reduction (gain)

✅ Exam Tips

  • ✅ Read ALL questions before starting

  • ✅ Manage time: Theory (1hr 30min), Objective (1hr)
