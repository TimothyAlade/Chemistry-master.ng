Chapter 6: Stoichiometry

📘 SS1 First Term⏱️ Reading: 40 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Understand the mole concept
  • Calculate molar mass
  • Balance chemical equations
  • Solve stoichiometric calculations
  • Determine empirical and molecular formulas

6.1 The Mole Concept

📌 Mole: The amount of substance that contains exactly 6.02 × 10²³ particles (Avogadro's number). This is the number of atoms in exactly 12g of carbon-12.
Number of moles (n) = Mass (g) / Molar mass (g/mol)
Number of particles = n × 6.02 × 10²³
📐 Example: How many moles in 36g of water (H₂O = 18 g/mol)?
n = 36/18 = 2 moles

6.2 Molar Mass

📌 Molar Mass: The mass of one mole of a substance. Calculated by adding the atomic masses of all atoms in the formula.
📐 Examples:
H₂O = (2×1) + 16 = 18 g/mol
CO₂ = 12 + (2×16) = 44 g/mol
NaCl = 23 + 35.5 = 58.5 g/mol
H₂SO₄ = (2×1) + 32 + (4×16) = 98 g/mol

6.3 Balancing Chemical Equations

Rules:

  1. Write the unbalanced equation
  2. Count atoms on each side
  3. Add coefficients to balance
  4. Never change subscripts!
📐 Example: H₂ + O₂ → H₂O
Balanced: 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

6.4 Stoichiometric Calculations

📐 Example: What mass of CO₂ is produced when 24g of carbon burns? (C + O₂ → CO₂)
Moles of C = 24/12 = 2 mol
From equation: 1C → 1CO₂, so 2 mol C → 2 mol CO₂
Mass CO₂ = 2 × 44 = 88g

6.5 Empirical and Molecular Formulas

📌 Empirical Formula: Simplest whole-number ratio of atoms.
📌 Molecular Formula: Actual number of atoms in a molecule.
📐 Example: A compound contains 40% C, 6.7% H, 53.3% O. Find empirical formula.
C: 40/12=3.33, H: 6.7/1=6.7, O: 53.3/16=3.33
Ratio: C:H:O = 1:2:1 → CH₂O (empirical formula)

📝 Chapter 6 Summary

  • 1 mole = 6.02×10²³ particles (Avogadro's number)
  • n = mass/molar mass
  • Balance equations before calculations
  • Empirical formula = simplest ratio; Molecular = actual
