Chapter 8: Organic Chemistry I

📗 SS2 First Term⏱️ Reading: 40 mins

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Define organic chemistry
  • Identify hydrocarbons and their types
  • Name alkanes, alkenes, and alkynes (IUPAC)
  • Understand isomerism
  • Describe functional groups

8.1 What is Organic Chemistry?

📌 Organic Chemistry: The study of carbon compounds (except CO, CO₂, carbonates, and cyanides). All organic compounds contain carbon, usually bonded to hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen, or other elements.

8.2 Hydrocarbons

📌 Hydrocarbons: Compounds containing ONLY carbon and hydrogen.
TypeBondsGeneral FormulaSuffix
AlkanesSingle (C-C)CₙH₂ₙ₊₂-ane
AlkenesDouble (C=C)CₙH₂ₙ-ene
AlkynesTriple (C≡C)CₙH₂ₙ₋₂-yne

8.4 Alkenes (Unsaturated)

Contain at least one C=C double bond. More reactive than alkanes.

📐 Examples: Ethene (C₂H₄), Propene (C₃H₆), Butene (C₄H₈)

8.5 Alkynes

Contain at least one C≡C triple bond. Even more reactive.

📐 Example: Ethyne (C₂H₂) — commonly called acetylene, used in welding.

🎯 WAEC/NECO: IUPAC naming, alkane/alkene/alkyne identification, and functional groups are frequently tested!

📝 Chapter 8 Summary

  • Organic chemistry = study of carbon compounds
  • Alkanes: single bonds (CₙH₂ₙ₊₂, suffix -ane)
  • Alkenes: double bonds (CₙH₂ₙ, suffix -ene)
  • Alkynes: triple bonds (CₙH₂ₙ₋₂, suffix -yne)
  • Functional groups: -OH (alcohol), -COOH (acid), -COO- (ester)
